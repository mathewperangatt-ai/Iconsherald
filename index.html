About IconsHerald

Recognition. Legacy.
Permanence.

Why IconsHerald Exists

Professional accomplishments often become fragmented.

Across social networks, news reports, publications, institutional websites, conference brochures, and newspaper archives — a lifetime of professional contribution is rarely gathered in one permanent place.

IconsHerald exists to preserve meaningful professional achievement within one curated, permanent record.

Recognition Matters

Meaningful careers deserve recognition — not only from peers, but from future generations.

Doctors who have healed thousands. Lawyers who have argued for justice. Researchers who have advanced the frontiers of knowledge. Teachers whose students now lead institutions. Artists whose work has shaped culture. Entrepreneurs who have built enterprises that employed hundreds of families.

Their contributions are real, their impact lasting — yet their stories are scattered, their records incomplete. IconsHerald exists to ensure those contributions are recognised, documented, and remembered.

Legacy Matters

A career should remain accessible long after websites disappear.

Social profiles become inactive. Newspaper archives are lost. Institutional websites are redesigned and records erased. A professional record built over forty years can become inaccessible within a decade of retirement.

IconsHerald is designed as a long-term archive of professional achievement. Profiles are hosted permanently. In Memoriam — IconsHerald's separate division for posthumous archives commissioned by families and institutions — is sealed at publication and never taken down.

Editorial Philosophy

Every profile is composed by the editorial team, reviewed, and member approved.

IconsHerald profiles are not self-written. Every profile is composed by the IconsHerald editorial team, with the assistance of AI-based editorial tools, and approved by the member before publication. Nothing appears without the member's explicit confirmation.

Acceptance into IconsHerald is not automatic. Every application is reviewed. Inclusion in the Register is intended for professionals with a demonstrable record of achievement, contribution, or distinction.

The standard is simple: does this career deserve to be documented and preserved? If the answer is yes, we want to do that work well.

Frequently Asked Questions
Can profiles be viewed in other languages? ▾
Modern browsers provide built-in translation capabilities allowing visitors to view profiles in their preferred language. All profiles are written and maintained in English. Future multilingual editorial support may be introduced.
Is acceptance into IconsHerald guaranteed? ▾
No. Submitting an application is an application for editorial profile creation, not an instant enrolment. Every application is reviewed by the IconsHerald editorial team. Inclusion in the Register is intended for professionals with a demonstrable record of achievement, contribution, or distinction. Acceptance is not automatic.
What happens after I submit an application? ▾
The editorial team reviews your application and, if suitable, approves it. After approval, you complete the editorial questionnaire for your selected package and the upfront payment (50% + GST) is collected. The editorial team then prepares your biography and shares your complete profile for approval. Once you approve, the balance payment is collected and your profile is published. Nothing is published without your explicit approval.
What does an editorial profile cost? ▾
Editorial Profile Creation is a one-time fee: Emerging at ₹6,000, Accomplished at ₹12,000, or Distinguished at ₹18,000 (all inclusive of GST). This single fee composes and publishes your profile. Separately, an Annual Profile Maintenance & Verification fee of ₹3,000 + GST per year preserves your published archive going forward. For Editorial Profile Creation, 50% of the fee plus applicable GST is payable upfront before editorial preparation begins, with the balance due on completion, after you approve your finished profile.
Why is there an annual fee if profile creation is one-time? ▾
The editorial creation fee covers composing and publishing your profile once. The Annual Profile Maintenance & Verification fee of ₹3,000 + GST per year is a separate, ongoing programme that covers secure hosting, permanent profile availability, archive preservation, QR code continuity, secure backups, search engine indexing maintenance, platform infrastructure, security monitoring, identity verification status, and minor technical corrections. It sustains the institution that keeps your profile dignified and permanently accessible — it does not produce a new biography.
How long does editorial preparation take? ▾
Editorial preparation is typically completed within approximately 2–4 weeks of receipt of the upfront payment, depending on package, profile complexity, and your responsiveness to editorial requests.
Is the upfront payment refundable? ▾
Editorial work begins only after the upfront payment (50% of the editorial creation fee + GST) has been received. Once editorial preparation has commenced, this upfront payment is non-refundable, as professional editorial services have already been performed.
Can I update my profile after publication? ▾
Yes. Publication is not the end of the relationship. A Minor Editorial Update (₹3,000 + GST) suits a new award, an appointment, a publication, or a similar addition. A Major Editorial Revision (₹8,000 + GST) suits a biography rewrite or significant career progression. Both are separate editorial services from Annual Profile Maintenance & Verification, scoped and quoted according to the work involved.
What is In Memoriam? ▾
In Memoriam is a wholly separate editorial division of IconsHerald, reserved exclusively for permanent archives of those who are no longer with us. It is commissioned by families, foundations, or institutions, never by the individual themselves, and is entirely distinct from the living-professional Register. Visit In Memoriam →
How does In Memoriam pricing work? ▾
An Editorial Memorial Archive is ₹25,000 + GST, and includes professional editorial creation, a permanent URL, a QR code, hosting, maintenance, and verification for three years. After three years, optional continuation is available for ₹3,000 + GST per year. Editorial revisions during the archive period — a Minor Editorial Update (₹3,000 + GST) or a Major Editorial Revision (₹8,000 + GST) — are arranged separately.
Which editorial package should I choose? ▾
Emerging, Accomplished, and Distinguished reflect three depths of editorial work, not a hierarchy of status. Emerging suits a concise professional profile; Accomplished adds awards, publications, and media coverage; Distinguished is the most comprehensive of the three for a living career, including books, research, and supporting documents.
How does profile approval work? ▾
Before publication, you review your complete profile — biography, timeline, awards, gallery, videos, documents, and overall layout — and must explicitly approve it. Your approval date, time, and the version approved are recorded. Nothing is published without this explicit confirmation.
Who owns the content of my profile? ▾
You retain ownership of the factual information and photographs you supply. IconsHerald retains copyright in its editorial composition, layout, and presentation of your profile.
What is the contact request facility? ▾
Your personal contact details are never displayed publicly without your consent. Visitors may securely initiate Contact Requests through the platform without your private contact information being exposed. Requests are forwarded to you, and you may independently reconnect through your own professional channels.
What is the difference between The IconsHerald Register and In Memoriam? ▾
The IconsHerald Register contains editorial profiles of living professionals. In Memoriam is a wholly separate editorial division, reserved exclusively for permanent archives commissioned by families, foundations, and institutions to honour individuals no longer with us. In Memoriam archives are sealed at publication and never taken down.
Is IconsHerald available internationally? ▾
Yes. IconsHerald is open to accomplished professionals anywhere in the world.

Founder
Mathew Varghese Perangatt
Founder, IconsHerald

Mathew Varghese Perangatt spent four decades navigating institutions and markets where professional reputation was everything — and observed throughout that most accomplished people had no adequate permanent record of what they had built.

His commercial career began in international banking, where he spent sixteen years at the National Bank of Dubai, progressing through trade finance and treasury operations to branch management. The banking environment of that era — its formality, its standards, its weight placed on professional standing and institutional credibility — shaped his understanding of what recognition meant and how rarely it was adequately documented.

He subsequently spent seventeen years as the commercial representative in various geographies for three international medical technology companies, navigating the full complexity of institutional procurement in regulated healthcare markets — government hospitals, academic medical centres, and regulatory authorities. These were careers built in rooms that most people never enter, on decisions whose consequences were measured in patient lives. The professionals who made those decisions — surgeons, scientists, administrators of genuine distinction — had no adequate permanent record of their contribution.

"I spent nearly four decades working alongside people who had built extraordinary professional careers — and I watched those careers go undocumented. Not out of modesty. Simply because there was no adequate place for them."

The idea that became IconsHerald emerged from a specific observation: the professionals whose careers most deserved documentation were, in many cases, the ones whose stories were least visible. Doctors and lawyers whose professional norms left little room for public self-presentation. Scientists whose work was technical or classified. Business leaders whose careers unfolded in sectors that general media never covered. These were not people without achievements. They were people without archives.

IconsHerald was created to be that archive — a platform designed around the proposition that a meaningful professional career deserves a permanent, editorially composed record that outlasts the institutions it passed through, the publications that briefly covered it, and the platforms that were never built to preserve it.
